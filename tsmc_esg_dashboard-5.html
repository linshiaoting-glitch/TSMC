Critical miss — unit GHG emissions

TSMC's per-unit GHG target (−30% vs 2020 by 2030) is the most critical off-track item. In 2024, the metric moved in the wrong direction (+19%), primarily because new advanced-node fabs (3nm, 2nm) consume disproportionately more energy per wafer layer during ramp-up. Without an acceleration in renewable procurement, this target will be structurally unachievable.

Partial decoupling — the key narrative

Revenue CAGR +18% vs GHG CAGR +13% from 2020–2024. Carbon intensity fell 15%. This is a real improvement — but absolute emissions still rose +65%. Investors will accept relative gains only temporarily; the SBTi 1.5°C pathway requires absolute cuts starting 2025.

The electricity problem

Scope 2 electricity is 52% of total GHG and growing fastest. In 2024 it reached 10.96M tCO₂e — a 47% increase from 2020. This single driver will prevent TSMC from meeting any of its 2030 climate targets unless renewable procurement accelerates from 14.1% to ≥60% by 2030. TSMC has signed 2.9 GW of PPAs but needs ~15 GW to meet its 60% target given projected energy growth.

Strategic challenges
Key ESG risks identified from data analysis

1 — Electricity decarbonization speed

Energy demand rising +8% per year. Renewables at 14.1% vs 60% target. Gap requires adding ~4 GW of new PPA annually through 2030 — TSMC has signed only 2.9 GW to date.

2 — Scope 3 opacity

Only partial categories disclosed. Bloomberg/CDP estimates total S3 at ~15.1M tCO₂e — nearly equal to S1+S2 combined. SBTi requires full S3 boundary reporting by 2026.

3 — Governance disclosure gaps

Gender pay gap, CEO pay ratio, and full turnover data remain undisclosed. DJSI World Index and MSCI AAA rating both flag these as "improvement required" items for 2025 re-assessment.

4 — Water in Arizona & Japan

New fab expansions in water-stressed Arizona and Kumamoto (groundwater dependent) create physical climate risk. TSMC restored 5M m³ in Kumamoto in 2024 — a positive step but not yet systematic.

Recommendations
Data-driven actions to close the gap

1 — Accelerate RE procurement plan

Current trajectory reaches ~22% renewable by 2030. To hit 60%, TSMC needs a published annual PPA addition target (≥4 GW/year) and on-site solar maximization across all Taiwan fabs by 2026.

2 — Publish full Scope 3 inventory by 2026

Categorize all 15 GHG Protocol categories. Cat 11 (Use of Sold Products) is TSMC's largest S3 source — the "4.28 kWh saved per kWh produced" metric is powerful and should anchor the S3 narrative.

3 — Close governance data gaps

Disclose adjusted gender pay gap, CEO-to-median pay ratio, and voluntary turnover rate in 2025 report. These are table-stakes for institutional ESG investors and are already tracked internally.

4 — Hardwire absolute GHG ceiling

Set a Scope 1+2 absolute cap at 2024 levels (~11.1M tCO₂e) as a Board-approved constraint. This sends a credible SBTi signal and forces new fabs to be built with renewables from day one.
